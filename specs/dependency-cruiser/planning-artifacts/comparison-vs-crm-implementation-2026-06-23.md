# dependency-cruiser: ui-toolkit plan vs CRM implementation

## 1. Executive verdict

The ui-toolkit plan is a faithful and largely sound adaptation of CRM's proven dependency-cruiser implementation: it ports all 12 of CRM's generic dependency-health rules and both type/runtime-split rules at preserved severities, drops the ~24 bulletproof-react layering/allowed-folders/naming rules with concrete and correct per-family justification, and replaces them with three components-centric rules suited to a published PascalCase component library. The resolution-critical options block (`tsPreCompilationDeps`, `combinedDependencies`, `enhancedResolveOptions`, `tsConfig.fileName`) is copied verbatim, the Makefile-as-single-source-of-truth and dedicated-CI-workflow patterns are preserved, and the central analytical insight — that ui-toolkit mirrors runtime libs into both `devDependencies` and `peerDependencies`, so a verbatim `not-to-dev-dep` port would fail the zero-tolerance gate on first run — is factually verified and correctly handled. The residual weaknesses are mechanical, not philosophical: two new rules specified by intent but lacking a concrete regex, a reporter naming inconsistency, and a small set of dropped workflow-hardening controls. Overall fidelity score: **88** (per-dimension average of 86 / 88 / 88 / 88 / 88).

## 3. Per-dimension detail

### 3.1 Rule set (lead dimension)

**What aligns.** All 12 of CRM's generic-health rules are ported by name and severity: `no-circular` (error), `no-orphans` (error), `no-deprecated-core` (warn), `not-to-deprecated` (warn), `no-non-package-json` (error), `not-to-unresolvable` (error), `no-duplicate-dep-types` (warn), `not-to-test` (error), `not-to-spec` (error), `not-to-dev-dep` (error), `optional-deps-used` (info), `peer-deps-used` (warn) — matching CRM exactly (Decision 3 rule table; Story 1-3 AC1/AC2). Both type/runtime-split rules — `type-files-imported-as-type-only` (error) and `type-files-no-runtime-imports` (error), CRM lines 520-553, the issue #88 rules — are ported at error severity (Story 1-3 AC6/Task 5); these are layout-agnostic and depend only on `tsPreCompilationDeps:true`, which the options block preserves. Story 1-3 Task 3.4 explicitly mandates verifying no advisory rule uses error severity, mirroring CRM's `warn`/`info` advisory band. The `no-orphans` allowlist requirements are grounded in real files (`src/index.ts`, `src/components/index.ts`, `src/types/styles.d.ts`, `src/components/Types.d.ts`, the 21 `*.stories.tsx`, and the `import './fonts.css'` side-effect in `src/components/index.ts` — all verified to exist).

**Divergences.**

- **`not-to-dev-dep` re-scoping (intentional, sound).** CRM forbids `src ↛ npm-dev` with `dependencyTypesNot:['type-only']` and `to.pathNot ['node_modules/@types/']` (CRM lines 169-189), relying on the premise that CRM runtime libs are true `dependencies`. That premise does not hold for ui-toolkit: all 9 runtime libs (`@emotion/react`, `@emotion/styled`, `@mui/material`, `@mui/system`, `react`, `react-dom`, `react-hook-form`, `i18next`, `react-i18next`) are declared under **both** `peerDependencies` and `devDependencies`, with `swiper` the only pure `dependency` (verified). A verbatim port would fire on 100+ MUI/React imports and fail the zero-tolerance gate on first run. The plan adds an `npm-peer` exclusion (Decision 3 dev+peer note; Story 1-3 AC1/Task 1.4). The `dependencyTypesNot:['npm-peer']` form is the more robust choice over a hand-maintained `pathNot` allowlist, because `combinedDependencies:true` (mirrored from CRM, Decision 2) means a dual-placed module resolves with both `npm-dev` and `npm-peer` tags, so excluding `npm-peer` reliably spares exactly the dual-placed set without enumerating it. CRM's existing `type-only` + `node_modules/@types/` exceptions are preserved and layered under, not discarded.

- **Layering rules dropped (CRM rules 13-36; intentional, sound).** All ~24 error-severity bulletproof-react rules (`no-cross-module-imports`, `no-components-import-modules`, `no-repository-internal-imports`, `no-repositories-to-ui-hooks`, `feature-hooks-file-convention`, `no-feature-direct-http-client`, `no-store-direct-http-client`, `no-feature-ui-to-services`, `no-di-config-import-outside-composition-root`, `no-cross-feature-imports`, `no-components-to-repositories`, `no-components-to-store`, `no-store-to-feature-ui`, `no-lib-to-features`, the module/feature/tests allowed-folders rules, `no-uppercase-paths`, and the `*-name-kebab-case` family) are dropped (Decision 3 "CRM rules explicitly dropped"; Story 1-3 Task 6). Justified per-family: `src/{hooks,lib,providers,routes,stores,utils}` are empty placeholders and no `modules/features/repositories` layout exists, so these rules would match nothing; PascalCase component dirs (`UiButton/`, `UiCardItem/ServicesHoverCard/`, both verified) mean `no-uppercase-paths` and `*-name-kebab-case` would fail the whole tree. This is the textbook correct library-vs-SPA divergence.

- **`components-public-api` (NEW barrel rule; intentional, questionable).** A new error rule forbidding reach-ins past a component's public entry (`index.tsx` OR `index.ts`) into another component's internals (Decision 3; Story 1-3 Task 4.3). Sound in intent and well-motivated (mixed `index.ts`/`index.tsx` barrels and nested dirs like `UiCardItem/ServicesHoverCard/ImageItem` are real), but the plan never gives the actual dependency-cruiser regex. CRM's nearest analogue, `no-repository-internal-imports`, uses a precise negative-lookahead (`^src/.../repositories/(?!index...).+`); the ui-toolkit rule is harder because component dirs nest arbitrarily, the barrel is sometimes `.ts` and sometimes `.tsx`, and a sibling subcomponent legitimately imports its own parent's internals. Without a concrete, tested pattern it risks over-firing on legitimate intra-component imports or under-catching cross-component reach-ins. This is the least-specified rule in the set.

- **`src-not-to-tests` vs `not-to-test` (intentional, questionable).** CRM expresses `src ↛ tests` once (`not-to-test`, CRM lines 138-153). The plan keeps `not-to-test` (FR5) and adds a separately-named `src-not-to-tests` (FR6) to satisfy two FRs, but articulates no behavioral difference. If both are authored with overlapping patterns they will double-report the same violation. Redundant rather than wrong.

- **`no-non-package-json` coverage (unintentional wording, sound).** CRM targets `dependencyTypes:['npm-no-pkg','npm-unknown']` (lines 94-108). The Decision 3 paraphrase "(dependencies/peer/optional)" is a loose description of which placements count as declared, not a change to `dependencyTypes`. Behavior is identical as long as the implementer copies CRM's targets verbatim rather than inventing a path-based rule.

- **`no-deprecated-core` path list (intentional, sound).** The plan keeps the rule (warn) but does not reproduce CRM's explicit deprecated-core path list (CRM lines 57-78). Acceptable: the rule is advisory and the list is standard scaffold; copy CRM's verbatim.

**Risks.** Medium: `components-public-api` has no concrete regex and either failure mode surfaces on the zero-tolerance first run. Low: `not-to-test`/`src-not-to-tests` double-reporting; `no-orphans` first-run failure if the allowlist is hand-listed instead of using a generic `\.d\.ts$` pattern (`src/react-app-env.d.ts` exists but is unnamed in the plan); `not-to-dev-dep` correctness drifting if a `pathNot` enumeration is used instead of `dependencyTypesNot:['npm-peer']`.

### 3.2 Config options block

**What aligns.** `tsConfig.fileName:'tsconfig.json'` (both; the plan correctly relies on `tsconfig.json` extending `tsconfig.paths.json` for `@/* -> ./src/*` and forbids pointing at `tsconfig.paths.json` directly — Story 1.2 AC4). `tsPreCompilationDeps:true` (CRM line 560), `combinedDependencies:true` (line 561), and `skipAnalysisNotInRules:true` (line 571) carried verbatim. `enhancedResolveOptions` copied byte-identically: `exportsFields:['exports']`, `conditionNames:['import','require','node','default','types']`, `extensions:['.ts','.tsx','.d.ts','.js']`, `mainFields:['main','types','typings']` (CRM lines 565-570). `doNotFollow` on `node_modules` and `reporterOptions.text.highlightFocused:true` (CRM line 595) carried over.

**Divergences.**

- **`detectProcessBuiltinModuleCalls` dropped (unintentional, questionable).** CRM sets `detectProcessBuiltinModuleCalls:true` (line 559); the plan omits it from both Decision 2 and Story 1.2 with no rationale. ui-toolkit `src` uses `process.env` (e.g. `VilnaCRMEmail.tsx`, `PrivacyPolicy.tsx`) as the global, not a `'process'` module import, so practical impact is near-zero — but it is a cheap, harmless flag dropped silently.
- **`options.exclude` added vs CLI `--exclude` (intentional, sound).** CRM has no `options.exclude`; it excludes `^\.stryker-tmp/` via a Makefile CLI flag and cruises `.`. The plan consolidates a broader regex (`build|.next|storybook-static|coverage|.stryker-tmp|playwright-report|test-results|reports|.lighthouseci|.qlty`) into config and cruises `src`. Cleaner for local/CI parity; mostly belt-and-suspenders given the `src` scope.
- **`builtInModules.add` dropped (intentional, sound).** CRM adds `bun:*`, `detect-libc`, `k6`, `k6/http` (lines 572-584). No such imports exist in `src` (`k6` appears only in `tests/load/README.md`, outside scope); adding them would be dead config.
- **dot/archi `collapsePattern` dropped (intentional, sound).** Decision 6 puts visual reporters out of PRD scope; carrying their collapse patterns would be inert.
- **`doNotFollow.path` string vs array (intentional, cosmetic).** dependency-cruiser accepts both; identical behavior.

**Risks.** Low: `detectProcessBuiltinModuleCalls` dropped without recorded rationale (reviewer cannot tell oversight from intent); `.stryker-tmp` entry redundant under `src` scope; resolution of a representative `@mui/*` type-only import under `tsPreCompilationDeps:true` should be verified during Story 1.2 Task 5 since the inherited `enhancedResolveOptions` are the only thing wiring it.

### 3.3 CLI invocation & Makefile / aggregate-lint wiring

**What aligns.** Makefile-as-single-source-of-truth preserved: CI runs `make lint-dep-cruiser`, never a raw `bun x depcruise` YAML line (Story 2-2 Task 5.2), mirroring CRM's `run: make lint-deps` (dependency-cruiser.yml line 28). Invocation routed through the docker `bun` service via `$(BUN_X)` (= `docker compose run --rm bun bun x`; ui-toolkit Makefile line 15 `BUN_X = $(BUN) x`), matching CRM's `$(BUNX)` (CRM line 103). Aggregate-lint membership preserved (plan appends `lint-dep-cruiser` to `lint:`, mirroring CRM line 346). `.PHONY` registration carried (Story 1-4 Task 2/AC2). Config-options parity backs the invocation so resolution behaves identically. Verified against the real ui-toolkit Makefile: `$(BUN_X)` expands to `docker compose run --rm bun bun x`; the `bun` service is the `build:.` image used by all lint targets.

**Divergences.**

- **Cruise scope `src` vs `.` (intentional, sound).** CRM cruises `.` (line 310), appropriate for an SPA where every layer is governed. The plan cruises `src`, rooted at `src/index.ts` (`export * from './components'`, verified) — correct for a library whose only real code is `src/components` + `src/types`. `tests/` is enforced as a forbidden target via `not-to-test`/`src-not-to-tests` rather than analyzed as source.
- **Exclusions in config vs CLI flag (intentional, sound).** Defensible normalization that strengthens local/CI parity; the recipe stays a bare `depcruise --config ... src`.
- **No `CI_LINT_TARGETS` split analogue (intentional, sound).** CRM keeps `lint-deps` in `lint:` but excludes it from `CI_LINT_TARGETS` (line 115) so its parallel runner never runs it; it runs only in its dedicated workflow. ui-toolkit has no `CI_LINT_TARGETS` or parallel runner, so the end state (in `lint:` locally, isolated in a dedicated CI workflow) matches CRM's intent; the mechanism differs only because there is no parallel runner to exclude from. Minor nit: the architecture never names CRM's `CI_LINT_TARGETS`-exclusion as the precedent.
- **Variable-chain documentation error (unintentional, questionable).** Story 1-4 Task 1.3 says `$(BUN_X)` resolves via the "`RUN_BUN_SH` / `BUN` / `BUN_X`" chain. The real chain is `RUN_BUN -> BUN -> BUN_X` (lines 10, 13, 15); `RUN_BUN_SH` (line 11) is a separate `--entrypoint sh` helper not in the lineage. The recipe and architecture doc are correct; only the prose is wrong.
- **Target rename `lint-dep-cruiser` vs `lint-deps` (intentional, sound).** More descriptive, consistent with ui-toolkit's own style (`lint-test-structure`, `format-check`).

**Risks.** All low: the Task 1.3 variable-chain mislabel; the un-cited `CI_LINT_TARGETS` precedent; partial redundancy between `src` scoping and the broad `options.exclude` list if scope is later widened.

### 3.4 CI workflow (`.github/workflows/dependency-cruiser.yml`)

**What aligns.** Split-out dedicated workflow preserved (Architecture Decision 5; Foundation Option 1), mirroring CRM's standalone `dependency cruiser` workflow and its `CI_LINT_TARGETS` exclusion. Trigger parity (`pull_request`, `branches: [main]`). Least-privilege `permissions: contents: read` (CRM workflow-level; plan job-level — equivalent for a single-job workflow). CI invokes the make target, never a raw CLI. `make start` bring-up and `runs-on: ubuntu-latest` preserved. Required-status-check model carried into Story 2.2 with an exact `dependency-cruiser` name-match contract so branch protection binds correctly.

**Divergences.**

- **`setup-node` dropped (intentional, sound).** CRM provisions a host Node via `actions/setup-node` + `node-version: ${{ vars.NODE_VERSION }}` (lines 19-22). ui-toolkit runs every gate inside the docker-compose `bun` service, so no host Node is needed — verified by zero `setup-node`/`NODE_VERSION` references across all 17 ui-toolkit workflows.
- **Tag-pinned checkout (intentional, questionable).** Plan uses `actions/checkout@v4` (matching the mirrored `static-testing.yml`); CRM SHA-pins (`@34e1148... # v4`), as do ui-toolkit's infra workflows (alpine-base-guard, dockerfile-performance, autorelease). Defensible by template consistency but the weaker of ui-toolkit's two house styles, forgoing pin immutability.
- **`make down` teardown added (intentional, sound).** `if: always() && steps.project.outputs.present == 'true'`, lifted from `static-testing.yml` — guarantees cleanup of the compose stack even on gate failure.
- **Bootstrap skip gate added (intentional, sound).** "Detect runtime project files" step gating `make start`/lint/`down` on `package.json` + `bun.lock` presence, mirroring `static-testing.yml`, keeps the workflow inert on bootstrap PRs.
- **`persist-credentials: false` dropped (unintentional, gap-or-error).** CRM sets it (lines 16-17), and sibling ui-toolkit workflows (integration-testing.yml:17, performance-testing.yml:21, alpine-base-guard, dockerfile-performance) set it; the plan inherited the weaker `static-testing.yml` variant by template accident. Low real-world risk (the job only reads and runs a lint gate), but a free hardening control silently dropped vs CRM.
- **No concurrency block (intentional, sound).** House-consistent (only dockerfile-performance.yml uses concurrency); low cost for a short docker-bracketed gate.

**Risks.** Medium: dropped `persist-credentials: false`. Low: mutable tag pin; no concurrency; required-check registration living only in Story 2.2 branch-protection (a rename without a branch-protection update silently un-blocks the gate — the same latent risk CRM carries).

### 3.5 Governed scope, enforcement model & reporting

**What aligns.** Forbidden-only, zero-tolerance posture with **no** depcruise-baseline — matches CRM, which ships none (verified: CRM's only "baseline" references are an unrelated eslint-suppression policy, Makefile ~line 349). Advisory severities preserved, not escalated (`no-deprecated-core`/`not-to-deprecated`/`no-duplicate-dep-types`/`peer-deps-used` = warn, `optional-deps-used` = info). Error rules fail the gate immediately and surface file+rule (FR11/FR15/FR17). Local/CI parity via a single make target on both sides (FR18); target folded into aggregate `make lint`. Generic-health and type-split rules ported faithfully. First-run-must-be-clean discipline mandated (resolve pre-existing violations in-slice, no baseline). ESLint complementarity grounded: `import/no-cycle` is `off` in `eslint.config.mjs` (line 287), so dependency-cruiser owning cycle detection is a genuine non-redundant gap (FR19). Deferred scope (rust-code-analysis #59, IDE integration, multi-repo reuse, dot/archi, auto-fix, baseline adoption) explicitly out of scope.

**Divergences.**

- **Governed scope root `src` vs `.` (intentional, sound).** Only `src/components` + `src/types` hold real code; narrowing keeps the graph meaningful. Trade-off: orphan/cycle hygiene inside `tests/` is not enforced, consistent with the library's stated scope.
- **`not-to-dev-dep` re-scope (intentional, sound, necessary).** As in 3.1: all 9 peer deps are also dev deps, `swiper` the only pure dependency; a verbatim port fails the whole tree on first run. Implementation must verify `dependencyTypesNot:['npm-peer']` actually suppresses these empirically before enabling the gate.
- **Reporter / output format (unintentional, questionable).** CRM's config sets no top-level reporter and relies on depcruise's default text/err behavior plus `reporterOptions.text.highlightFocused:true`. The plan is internally inconsistent: PRD/Decision 2 describe "default text reporter," Decision 6 names the `err` reporter. Both exit non-zero on error and name file+rule (so FR15/FR17 holds either way), but `err` suppresses non-erroring info/warn lines — which would **hide** the deliberately-kept advisory warnings (`peer-deps-used`, `not-to-deprecated`, `no-duplicate-dep-types`, `optional-deps-used`). If those warns are meant to be visible, the config should use the default/text reporter.
- **Build/report exclusion mechanism (intentional, sound).** Config `options.exclude` vs CRM's CLI `--exclude` — cleaner, stronger parity, expanded list matches ui-toolkit's actual generated dirs.
- **`no-orphans` generated-dir allowlist (intentional, sound).** CRM lists generated dirs in `no-orphans` `pathNot`; the plan instead removes them via `options.exclude`, so duplicating them in the orphan allowlist would be dead config. The plan correctly targets this tree's real orphan risks (entry barrels, `fonts.css`, `.d.ts`, 21 stories).

**Risks.** High: `not-to-dev-dep` peer-dep suppression asserted but unverified against depcruise's actual dependency-type classification — if the chosen exclusion does not fire, the first run fails on 100+ imports and blocks the tree. Medium: reporter ambiguity hiding advisory warns in CI logs. Low: orphan-allowlist completeness depending on regex coverage; checkout tag-pin + missing `persist-credentials:false` as a supply-chain regression vs CRM.

## 4. Intentional & justified divergences

These are deliberate, defensible adaptations for a published component library (not gaps):

1. **Dropped bulletproof-react layering rules** (~24: `no-cross-module-imports` through the allowed-folders rules) — the `modules/features/repositories/stores` layout they target does not exist in ui-toolkit.
2. **Dropped naming rules incl. `no-uppercase-paths` and `*-name-kebab-case`** — component dirs are PascalCase, so any lowercase/kebab rule would fail the whole tree.
3. **Added components-centric rules** — `src-not-to-tests`, `no-prod-import-of-stories`, `components-public-api` — encode this library's real boundaries (21 stories, mixed `index.ts`/`index.tsx` barrels, nested component dirs), which CRM has no analogue for.
4. **`not-to-dev-dep` re-scoped with an `npm-peer` exclusion** — for the dev+peer overlap (9 dual-placed libs; `swiper` the only pure dependency), without which a verbatim port fails the zero-tolerance gate on first run.
5. **Docker-runtime CI instead of `setup-node`** — every gate runs inside the docker-compose `bun` service, so a host Node runtime is dead weight.
6. **`src/` graph scope vs cruising `.`** — only `src/components` + `src/types` hold real code; `tests/` is enforced as a forbidden target, not analyzed as source.
7. **Exclusions consolidated into config `options.exclude`** vs CRM's CLI `--exclude` flag — strengthens local/CI parity.
8. **Dropped `builtInModules.add` and dot/archi reporter options** — no `bun:*`/`k6`/`detect-libc` imports in `src`, and visual reporters are out of PRD scope; both would be inert config.

## 5. Gaps, errors, or risks (ranked by severity)

1. **HIGH — `not-to-dev-dep` peer-dep suppression unverified.** The plan asserts the dev+peer overlap is spared but offers two mechanisms (`to.pathNot` allowlist OR `dependencyTypesNot:['npm-peer']`) without committing to one or empirically confirming depcruise's classification. If the chosen exclusion does not fire, the first zero-tolerance run fails on 100+ MUI/React/i18next imports and blocks the entire tree.
2. **MEDIUM — `components-public-api` has no concrete regex.** Arbitrary nesting, mixed `.ts`/`.tsx` barrels, and legitimate intra-component imports make a naive pattern over-fire or under-catch — either surfacing on the first run.
3. **MEDIUM — reporter ambiguity (`err` vs default/text).** Decision 2 and Decision 6 disagree; choosing `err` would hide the deliberately-kept advisory warns from CI logs, undercutting the rationale for keeping them.
4. **MEDIUM — `persist-credentials: false` dropped** from the checkout step despite CRM and sibling ui-toolkit workflows setting it; a free hardening control silently lost by template accident.
5. **LOW — `not-to-test` / `src-not-to-tests` overlap** will double-report `src -> tests` if authored with overlapping patterns; the split exists only to map FR5/FR6 separately.
6. **LOW — `no-orphans` allowlist completeness.** `src/react-app-env.d.ts` exists but is unnamed; a hand-listed allowlist (instead of a generic `\.d\.ts$` regex) would orphan-fail the first run.
7. **LOW — `detectProcessBuiltinModuleCalls:true` silently omitted** vs CRM, with no recorded rationale.
8. **LOW — checkout tag-pin** (`@v4`) vs CRM's SHA pin; a mutable-pin supply-chain regression, though consistent with `static-testing.yml`.
9. **LOW — Story 1-4 Task 1.3 variable-chain mislabel** (`RUN_BUN_SH / BUN / BUN_X` should be `RUN_BUN / BUN / BUN_X`).
10. **LOW — required-check registration** lives only in Story 2.2 branch-protection; a workflow/job rename without a matching update silently un-blocks the gate.

## 6. Recommendations (ordered)

1. **Empirically verify `not-to-dev-dep` before enabling the gate.** Run `make lint-dep-cruiser` against current `main` with `dependencyTypesNot:['npm-peer']` (preferred over a drifting `pathNot` enumeration) and confirm **zero** `not-to-dev-dep` hits on the 9 dev+peer modules. Add a test asserting the `swiper`-only-true-dependency invariant. (Addresses risk 1; AC7/Task 7.1.)
2. **Author and test a concrete `components-public-api` regex.** Model it on CRM's `no-repository-internal-imports` negative-lookahead (`^src/components/<Name>/...(?!index\.tsx?$)...`), scoping `from` to OTHER components so a component reaching into its own internals is allowed. Validate against the real tree (esp. `UiCardItem/ServicesHoverCard` and `UiFooter` subdirs). (Addresses risk 2.)
3. **Pin the reporter explicitly and reconcile Decision 2 vs Decision 6.** Use the default/text reporter if advisory warns are meant to be visible; do not use `err` unless the warns are intentionally suppressed. (Addresses risk 3.)
4. **Add `with: { persist-credentials: false }`** to the `actions/checkout@v4` step in both the architecture's canonical YAML and Story 2.1 AC7/Task 2.1, restoring parity with CRM and the hardened ui-toolkit siblings. (Addresses risk 4.)
5. **Resolve the `not-to-test` / `src-not-to-tests` overlap** — give the two rules distinct non-overlapping scopes, or collapse into one and map both FR5 and FR6 to it. (Addresses risk 5.)
6. **Implement allowlist exemptions as regex classes** (`\.d\.ts$`, `\.stories\.tsx$`, `fonts.css`, `^src/(components/)?index\.(ts|tsx)$`) rather than enumerated paths, ensuring `react-app-env.d.ts` is covered; run the gate against `main` to confirm a clean baseline before registering the required check. (Addresses risk 6.)
7. **Add `detectProcessBuiltinModuleCalls:true` for direct CRM parity** (harmless for a browser/library codebase), or record a one-line rationale for the drop in Decision 2 / Story 1.2. (Addresses risk 7.)
8. **Correct Story 1-4 Task 1.3** to read `RUN_BUN / BUN / BUN_X` (or just "the existing `BUN_X` variable"); the recipe needs no change. (Addresses risk 9.)
9. **Optionally** SHA-pin the checkout and add a `concurrency` group keyed on `github.ref` with `cancel-in-progress: true` if the repo later standardizes on hardened required-check workflows. (Addresses risks 8 and the concurrency note.)
10. **Keep the Story 2.2 exact-name contract and V.2 verification** that the registered branch-protection check name matches the workflow/job name; treat any rename as a breaking change. (Addresses risk 10.)

## 7. Addendum — decisions taken after this review (2026-06-23)

This report is a point-in-time analysis; the planning artifacts were subsequently revised, which **supersedes two of its conclusions**:

- **Naming rules reinstated (reverses §3.1 / §4 item 1-2).** Per stakeholder direction, ui-toolkit now adopts CRM's all-lowercase **kebab-case convention for every file and folder**. `no-uppercase-paths` and a kebab-case `*-name` rule family are no longer dropped — they are added to the architecture rule table (Decision 3) and governed by new **FR21–FR24**, a new **Decision 7 (Kebab-case Path Normalization & Migration)**, and a new **Epic 4** (stories 4.1–4.4).
